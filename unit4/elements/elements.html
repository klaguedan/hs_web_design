Elements of Design

by Katrina Leanne

Posted March 21, 2019

A website relies on all four elements of design—graphics, text, color, and layout—to become an effective tool for communication between the designer and the audience. However, as it is the foundational aspect that all other elements are embedded into, the layout of a page has the most importance.

It serves as a way for the webpage visitor to, figuratively and literally, navigate the information provided to them. For example, a standard convention for websites is to have a navigation bar with links to other pages. This is a feature I’ve implemented to my portfolio website and all its projects to help group related links and separate them by topic.

In the case of a standalone webpage with no hyperlinks, it should at least have clearly defined sections such as a main title, subtitles where appropriate, and plain text to visually categorize content for the reader. At a smaller scope, tables also lay out information in an efficient manner.

Any layout has a purpose to provide organization and stability within a design and thus transforms the nonsense of bare computer code into a comprehensive message.
